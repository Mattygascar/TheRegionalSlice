New York Style

New York–style pizza is known for large, foldable slices with a thin-to-medium crust, a crisp underside, and a chewy bite. It’s usually baked hot and fast, then served by the slice.

Quick Tips

  • Stretch the dough thin and keep the center slightly thinner than the rim.
  • Use a hot surface (pizza steel/stone if you have one) to get that crisp bottom.
  • Keep toppings light—NY slices are about balance and foldability.

NY Slice Basics Table

This table compares common traits you’ll see in New York–style slices.

Crust Thin to medium, foldable Easy to eat by the slice
