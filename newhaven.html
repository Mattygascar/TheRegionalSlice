New Haven Style

New Haven-style pizza, also called "apizza," is known for its thin crust, coal-fired bake, and slightly charred, crispy edges.

History

New Haven “apizza” dates back to Italian immigrants in the early 1900s. Coal-fired ovens became central to the style, giving the crust its signature char and smoky flavor.

Why It’s Different

New Haven pizza is known for its crisp yet tender crust and minimal cheese. It often features simple toppings like clams or olive oil, emphasizing balance and texture rather than heaviness.

Key Features

  • Coal-fired oven
  • Thin, crisp crust
  • Light cheese application
